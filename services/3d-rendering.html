What is 3D Rendering?

3D rendering is the computer-graphics process of converting 3D wireframe models into high-fidelity 2D images. Acting as a virtual camera, rendering calculates lighting angles, physical material specs, and lens apertures to output photorealistic visuals for commercial use.

At Vertex XR, our rendering pipeline simulates real-world path-tracing. This matches the exact ways that photons bounce off brushed metals, leather fibers, matte plastics, and glossy surfaces, creating depth-of-field and organic shadow roll-offs.

Light Path-Tracing Calculations

Our render farms calculate true physical light paths. We resolve complex light scatterings, soft ambient occlusions, secondary bounces, and specular caustics so materials look organic and tactile, avoiding flat artificial CGI styles.
